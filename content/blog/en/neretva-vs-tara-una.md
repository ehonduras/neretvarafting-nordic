If you are planning a Balkan trip and want to add rafting, you will quickly encounter three names: Neretva, Tara, and Una. All three offer beautiful nature and exciting rapids, but they suit different travel routes and schedules. This comparison helps you choose the right river for your trip — especially if you fly to Sarajevo or Mostar and want a smooth day trip without replanning your whole journey.

## The three rivers in brief

**Neretva** flows through central Bosnia and Herzegovina. The most popular rafting section runs from Glavatičevo to Konjic — about 23 kilometres with class II–IV rapids. Konjic sits midway between Sarajevo and Mostar.

**Tara** lies in Republika Srpska, near the border with Montenegro. Many consider it the wildest river in the region, with deep canyons and class III–IV rapids. The nearest major town is Foča, about two hours from Sarajevo.

**Una** flows in north-western Bosnia, near the Croatian border. It is known for crystal-clear turquoise water and waterfalls. The nearest town is Bihać, roughly three hours from Sarajevo.

All three are fantastic experiences. But location decides everything.

## Logistics: where you actually are

This is the decisive question for most international travellers. You fly to Sarajevo or Mostar, perhaps plan three to five days in the region, and want to add an active day. Which river requires the least replanning?

**Neretva wins clearly on logistics.** Konjic is 45 minutes from Sarajevo and one hour from Mostar. You can do a full day trip without changing your accommodation or route. Many travellers stay in Sarajevo, drive to Konjic in the morning, raft all day, and are back for dinner.

Tara requires a significantly longer journey — at least two hours one way from Sarajevo to Foča, plus transfer to the river. It works, but it becomes a long day with a lot of driving. If you are not already planning to visit eastern Bosnia, it is hard to justify as a day trip.

Una is even farther — Bihać is about three hours from Sarajevo in the opposite direction from Mostar. It fits best if your route goes via north-western Bosnia or Croatia, not as an add-on to a classic Sarajevo–Mostar trip.

## Experience and difficulty

Tara has a reputation as the most intense river, with steep canyons and powerful rapids. It is a fantastic experience — if you have time and are prepared for a full day with a long transfer.

The Neretva offers a more balanced day. You get action-packed class II–IV sections, but also calmer stretches where you can enjoy the scenery. The river runs through green valleys with mountains on both sides, and the water shifts from turquoise to deep green. It is a complete full-day experience with BBQ lunch on the riverbank — not just rapids, but a whole day in nature.

Una is the most photogenic river, with waterfalls and almost unreal blue water. The rapids are somewhat calmer (class II–III), which makes it good for families but less exciting for those seeking more action.

## Price and what's included

Prices are comparable across the three rivers:

- **Neretva full day** – around 50 EUR, includes equipment, IRF guide, BBQ lunch, and transfer
- **Combination packages** – around 45 EUR when booking multiple activities
- **Shorter experiences** – from around 35 EUR

Tara and Una prices fall in the same range, but added transport costs and possible extra overnight stays make the total cost higher if you are not already nearby.

## Best for your Balkan route

If your trip follows the classic international Balkan itinerary — fly to Sarajevo, a few days in the city, on to Mostar, perhaps Mostar as a base — the Neretva is the obvious choice. It works as a day trip without disrupting the rest of your plan.

Konjic is also an interesting town in its own right, with its historic wooden bridge and riverside setting. You can combine rafting with a short town walk if you have time after the trip.

Tara fits best if you plan a longer trip through eastern Bosnia or combine with Montenegro. Una fits if your route goes via the Bihać area or north-western Bosnia.

## Our tip for international travellers

We recommend the Neretva for most people travelling to the Sarajevo/Mostar area. You get a complete full day with IRF-certified guides, class II–IV rapids, BBQ lunch, and minimal travel time — all for around 50 EUR.

Save Tara and Una for your next Balkan trip, when you have more time and a route that passes closer. This trip — let the Neretva be your river day.
