When you travel to Bosnia and Herzegovina for the first time, it is natural to have questions about safety. Many of us are used to high standards in outdoor and adventure travel — and that is a good thing. Asking about safety is not being cautious, it is being smart. This guide covers exactly what you can expect from rafting on the Neretva, and why thousands of European travellers choose this section every year.

## Bosnia today — context for travellers

Bosnia and Herzegovina has changed dramatically since the 1990s. The country is now a stable destination receiving more visitors from across Europe each year. Sarajevo and Mostar are well-developed tourist cities with good infrastructure, and adventure activities like rafting have professional operators with international experience.

This is not about ignoring history — it is about understanding that today's Bosnia offers the same kind of organised, safe outdoor experiences you find in Norway, Austria, or Slovenia. The difference is price and landscape.

## IRF-certified guides — your most important safety guarantee

The single most important factor for safe rafting is the guide. On the Neretva, operators work with guides certified by the International Rafting Federation (IRF). IRF is the global standard for rafting guides and means the guide has trained in:

- Rapid assessment and water reading
- Rescue techniques on water and land
- Group management under stress
- First aid and emergency situations

An IRF guide knows exactly which sections are suitable for your group and which should be run differently depending on water level. They make decisions in real time — and they have done this thousands of times.

You are not just paying for a boat ride — you are paying for professional leadership.

## Equipment and standards

All equipment provided on site follows international safety requirements:

- **Life jackets** — approved to European standards, sized to your weight
- **Helmets** — mandatory for the entire trip, CE-marked
- **Wetsuits** — full-body, 3–5 mm, protecting against cold water and scrapes
- **Raft boats** — professional multi-chamber rafts designed for whitewater

You do not need to bring your own gear. Operators check every equipment set before each trip. If anything is worn or damaged it is replaced — just as you would expect from a serious outfitter in Europe.

## Rapids class II–IV — what does it mean?

The Neretva section between Glavatičevo and Konjic includes sections from class II to IV depending on water level and season. For perspective:

- **Class II** — easy rapids with clear passages, suitable for beginners
- **Class III** — moderately challenging, requires teamwork and listening to the guide
- **Class IV** — technically demanding, run only in suitable conditions with experienced groups

Most commercial trips on the Neretva focus on class II–III, with occasional class IV sections during higher water. The guide adapts the route to the group's experience and that day's conditions. You will never enter a rapid the guide has not judged safe for your group.

## Insurance — what to consider

Your regular travel insurance usually covers adventure activities like rafting, but always check the terms before departure. Specifically, verify:

- That **adventure sports coverage** is included
- That **medical evacuation** is covered in Bosnia and Herzegovina
- That **accidents on organised activities** are not excluded

Most European travel insurers cover rafting in Europe without an extra add-on, but it varies between policies. Call and ask if you are unsure.

Operators on the Neretva have their own liability insurance for the business, but your personal travel insurance is what protects you for personal injury or medical costs.

## Safety routines during the trip

Before you leave the shore the guide runs a mandatory briefing. You learn how to sit in the boat, how to paddle, what to do if you end up in the water, and which commands the guide uses. Listen actively — this is not a formality.

During the trip the guide always sits at the back of the boat and steers. You and the other participants paddle on command. At harder sections the guide stops and scouts the rapid before you run it. If conditions are judged unsafe, the trip is rerouted — no unnecessary risks are taken.

Lunch on the riverbank is a break where you can dry off and warm up. The BBQ meal is served at a safe spot away from the waterline.

## Why international travellers choose the Neretva

Travellers who come to Bosnia for rafting often return with the same verdict: professional, well organised, and good value. You get IRF-standard guiding at a fraction of what a similar experience costs in the Alps.

Konjic and Glavatičevo have established operators with season after season of experience. This is not a new trend — commercial rafting on the Neretva has run for over two decades.
